# Module 3: Hydropower Plant Types

## Overview
This module explores the different categories of hydropower plants and their specific characteristics, operational modes, and suitable applications.

## Run-of-River Hydropower

### System Characteristics
- Minimal water storage
- Continuous flow operation
- Limited daily variation
- Seasonal flow dependency

### Advantages
- Lower environmental impact
- Faster permitting
- Lower construction costs
- Predictable generation patterns

### Limitations
- Seasonal variability
- Limited flexibility
- Storage capacity constraints
- Water rights issues

### Applications
- Alpine rivers
- Continuous flow streams
- Small-to-medium scale projects
- Environmental preservation areas

## Storage Hydropower (Reservoir-Based)

### System Characteristics
- Large reservoir storage
- Variable output capability
- Multi-season operation
- Significant head development

### Advantages
- Dispatchable generation
- Load following capability
- Flood control benefits
- Recreational opportunities
- Water supply management

### Challenges
- Environmental impacts
- Large capital investment
- Long permitting timeline
- Sediment management

### Applications
- Large-scale projects
- System load management
- Irrigation support
- Recreation and tourism

## Pumped Storage Hydropower

### System Characteristics
- Dual reservoirs (upper and lower)
- Pumping and generating cycles
- Energy storage capability
- Grid support services

### Operating Cycle
- Off-peak pumping
- Peak period generation
- Round-trip efficiency
- Cycling limitations

### Grid Services
- Load shifting
- Frequency regulation
- Voltage support
- Reserve capacity

### Economic Viability
- Wholesale price arbitrage
- Ancillary service revenue
- System reliability benefits
- Long-term economics

## Small-Scale Hydropower

### Micro-Hydro Systems
- Head and flow variations
- Minimal civil works
- Local supply focus
- Environmental benefits

### Mini-Hydro Plants
- 100 kW to 1 MW range
- Community ownership
- Local economic development
- Technical simplicity

### Pico-Hydro Systems
- Under 5 kW capacity
- Off-grid applications
- Simple technology
- Accessibility in remote areas

## Seasonal and Tidal Hydropower

### Seasonal Variations
- Monsoon-driven systems
- Glacial melt operations
- Snowmelt hydropower
- Climate adaptability

### Tidal Energy Systems
- Tidal stream turbines
- Tidal barrages
- Predictability advantages
- Emerging technology

## System Comparison and Selection

### Performance Metrics
- Capacity factor
- Energy yield
- Flexibility
- Reliability

### Selection Criteria
- Site characteristics
- Resource availability
- Environmental considerations
- Regulatory framework
- Economic feasibility

## Learning Outcomes
You will understand:
- Characteristics of different hydropower plant types
- Operational modes and flexibility options
- Advantages and limitations of each type
- Suitable applications and contexts
- System selection and design considerations
