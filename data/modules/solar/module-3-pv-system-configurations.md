# Module 3: Photovoltaic System Configurations

## Overview
This module explores the different system configurations for photovoltaic installations and their applications in various settings.

### On-Grid Systems
- Direct grid connection
- No battery storage
- Bi-directional power flow
- Net metering capability
- Utility interconnection

### System Components
- Array and mounting
- String or central inverter
- Disconnect and protection
- Net meter
- Monitoring system

### Operating Characteristics
- Peak generation during daytime
- Zero generation at night
- Seasonal variations
- Grid dependency
- Utility rate structures

### Advantages
- Lower cost (no batteries)
- Simpler installation
- Utility backup available
- Minimal maintenance
- Net metering benefits

### Challenges
- Intermittency issues
- Grid dependency
- Interconnection requirements
- Utility rate variability
- Net metering uncertainty

## Off-Grid Systems

### Standalone Systems
- No grid connection
- Battery energy storage
- Charge controllers
- Backup generators (optional)
- Autonomous operation

### System Components
- Solar array (oversized)
- Battery bank
- Charge controller
- DC/AC inverter
- Backup generation
- Monitoring and control

### Sizing Considerations
- Load requirements
- Days of autonomy
- Battery capacity
- Array capacity
- System redundancy

### Applications
- Remote locations
- Cabins and cottages
- Telecommunications
- Water pumping
- Rural electrification

### Advantages
- Energy independence
- No utility dependence
- Reliability in remote areas
- Scalability
- Customizable solutions

### Challenges
- High initial cost
- Battery maintenance
- Complex system design
- Backup generator requirements
- System management complexity

## Hybrid Systems

### Grid-Connected with Battery Storage
- Grid connection capability
- Battery backup during outages
- Daytime grid export
- Night-time battery utilization
- Peak shaving ability

### Operating Modes
- Normal grid-connected operation
- Battery charging from excess generation
- Battery discharging during peak demand
- Backup during grid outage
- Load management and optimization

### System Management
- Smart energy management
- Demand forecasting
- Real-time optimization
- Islanding capability
- Seamless mode switching

### Applications
- Residential backup power
- Commercial peak shaving
- Demand charge management
- Critical load protection
- Enhanced resilience

## Rooftop Systems

### Residential Systems
- Single-family homes
- Roof-mounted arrays
- String inverters or micro-inverters
- Net metering advantage
- Typical 3-10 kW capacity

### Commercial and Industrial
- Business facilities
- Warehouse roofs
- Large arrays
- Central or string inverters
- 10 kW to several MW range

### System Design
- Roof assessment
- Structural analysis
- Shading evaluation
- Electrical integration
- Aesthetic considerations

### Challenges
- Roof condition and age
- Warranty implications
- Maintenance access
- Seasonal variation
- Space limitations

## Ground-Mounted Systems

### Fixed Installations
- Ground-based structures
- Landscape flexibility
- Maintenance accessibility
- Land use requirements
- Typical larger scale

### Tracking Systems
- Single-axis tracking
- Dual-axis tracking
- Solar irradiance optimization
- Increased generation
- Higher cost and complexity

### Utility-Scale Systems
- 1 MW and larger
- Large ground footprints
- Central inverters
- Advanced monitoring
- Grid-scale application

### Land Use
- Agricultural compatibility
- Agrivoltaics
- Dual-use potential
- Environmental impact
- Restoration after decommissioning

## Building-Integrated PV (BIPV)

### Architectural Integration
- Roof-integrated systems
- Facade-mounted arrays
- Window and skylight integration
- Aesthetically integrated
- Building envelope replacement

### Advantages
- Dual functionality (generation + building element)
- Space efficiency
- Aesthetic appeal
- Material savings potential
- Design flexibility

### Challenges
- Design complexity
- Material specifications
- Performance variability
- Higher costs
- Installation expertise required
