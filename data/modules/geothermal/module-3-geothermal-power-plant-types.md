# Module 3: Geothermal Power Plant Types

## Overview
This module explores the different categories of geothermal power plants and their specific characteristics, operational modes, and suitable applications.

## Flash Steam Power Plants

### System Characteristics
- High-temperature steam resources (>150°C)
- Single-flash systems
- Double-flash systems
- Triple-flash systems
- Pressure reduction and steam separation

### Operating Principles
- Reservoir fluid flashing
- Steam separation
- Turbine expansion
- Condenser operation
- Reservoir pressure management

### Advantages
- Proven technology
- High efficiency potential
- Suitable for high-temperature systems
- Scalability
- Operational flexibility

### Limitations
- Requires high-temperature resources
- Dissolved minerals in residual water
- Scaling and corrosion challenges
- Non-condensable gas handling
- More complex than binary systems

### Applications
- Geothermal fields with high temperatures
- Regions with mature resource development
- Grid-connected electricity generation
- Co-generation possibilities

## Binary Power Plants

### System Characteristics
- Moderate temperature resources (90-150°C)
- Closed-loop secondary circuit
- Working fluid with lower boiling point
- Heat exchanger between fluids
- Separated primary and secondary fluids

### Operating Principles
- Geothermal fluid as heat source
- Heat transfer to secondary fluid
- Secondary fluid evaporation
- Turbine expansion
- Cooling and recirculation

### Advantages
- Works with moderate temperatures
- Minimal non-condensable gas issues
- Reduced scaling and corrosion
- Simplified operation and maintenance
- Closed-loop system environmental benefits
- Lower emission potential

### Limitations
- Lower efficiency than flash systems
- Heat exchanger capital costs
- Working fluid environmental considerations
- Smaller typical sizes
- More limited scalability

### Working Fluid Selection
- Isobutane
- Isopentane
- Refrigerants
- Environmental impact considerations
- Cycle efficiency optimization

### Applications
- Moderate-temperature resources
- Expanding geographic range
- Flexible sizing
- Enhanced environmental protection

## Ground Source Heat Pump Systems

### System Characteristics
- Low-temperature applications
- Shallow to moderate depths
- Closed-loop operation
- Heating and cooling capability
- Individual or district scale

### Operating Principles
- Ground as heat source/sink
- Heat pump thermodynamic cycle
- Heating mode operation
- Cooling mode operation
- Seasonal flexibility

### Advantages
- Applicability almost everywhere
- Small land footprint
- Combined heating and cooling
- Long operational life
- Low operating costs

### Applications
- Building heating and cooling
- Widespread geographic distribution
- New construction integration
- Retrofit applications
- Distributed generation
